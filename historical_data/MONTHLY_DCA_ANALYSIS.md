## Why These Results?

### Why DCA Excels at 10 Years:

1. **Crash Timing**: 10-year windows often capture 1-2 complete market cycles
2. **Buying Opportunity**: More capital deployed DURING crashes (not just holding through them)
3. **Recovery Participation**: Later purchases ride full recovery with fresh capital
4. **Optimal Window**: Long enough to recover, short enough that DCA still deploying capital during crashes

### Why Lump-Sum Wins at 5 Years:

1. **Time in Market**: Immediate full exposure to any gains
2. **Short DCA Period**: Only 5 years of purchases, less averaging benefit
3. **Bull Market Capture**: If it's a bull market, lump-sum gets full 5 years of gains
4. **Compounding**: Extra years of compounding on full principal

### Why Lump-Sum Wins at 15 Years:

1. **Long Recovery Time**: Even bad entries recover over 15 years
2. **Compounding Dominates**: Immediate full exposure compounds for entire period
3. **DCA Drag**: DCA holds cash for up to 15 years (opportunity cost)
4. **Market History**: Markets tend up over long periods, favoring full exposure

## Real-World Example: The 2008 Crisis

### Scenario: Start investing in early 2007, hold through 2012 (5 years)

**Lump-Sum Strategy** ($30,000 in Jan 2007):
- Invested at market peak

**Monthly DCA** ($500/month starting Jan 2007):
- Jan 2007 - Sep 2008: Buying as market rose then fell (21 months)

- Apr 2009 - Dec 2011: Buying during recovery (33 months)
- Result: Average cost MUCH lower, more shares purchased

**Winner**: Depends on exact dates, but DCA typically better when crash happens mid-period!

## Key Takeaways

### 1. **DCA's 10-Year Sweet Spot**
- At 10 years, DCA reduces worst-case gap by **2.42%/year**
- This is the optimal window for dollar-cost averaging with leverage
- Captures crashes mid-period while still having time to recover

### 2. **Risk Reduction vs Return Maximization**
- **DCA**: Lower probability of bad outcomes, lower average returns
- **Lump-Sum**: Higher average returns, higher variance

### 3. **Market Timing Matters (for Lump-Sum)**
- Lump-sum is highly sensitive to entry point
- DCA eliminates entry point risk
- If you can't time the market, DCA is safer

### 4. **Leverage Amplifies Everything**
- Good DCA timing: Huge benefit (buying crashes cheap)
- Bad DCA timing: Still underperforms (buying peaks frequently)
- Leveraged ETFs make these effects more extreme

### 5. **Horizon Matters**
- **5 years**: Slight lump-sum advantage in worst case
- **10 years**: Clear DCA advantage (2.42%/year)
- **15+ years**: Lump-sum wins (time heals bad entries)
