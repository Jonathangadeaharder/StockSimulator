This analysis compares **three investment strategies** across all major indices:

1. **DCA 2x Leverage**: $500/month into 2x leveraged ETF
2. **Lump-Sum 2x Leverage**: Full amount into 2x leveraged ETF on Day 1
3. **Non-Leveraged**: Full amount into unleveraged index on Day 1 (baseline)

## üèÅ Final Recommendations

### By Market Type:

**Stable Growth (S&P 500, DJIA)**:
- Default: Lump-Sum 2x Leverage (90%+ win rate long-term)
- Exception: During obvious crashes, consider non-leveraged

**High Volatility (NASDAQ)**:
- Default: Lump-Sum 2x Leverage (80% win rate)
- BUT: If near peak/bubble, use DCA 2x or non-leveraged
- Timing matters MORE here

**Structural Decline Risk (Nikkei, Emerging Markets)**:
- Conservative: Non-Leveraged
- Moderate: DCA 2x Leverage
- Aggressive: Lump-Sum 2x (but accept 15-20% failure rate)

## üí∞ The Bottom Line

**Across all datasets, Lump-Sum 2x Leverage wins 2/3 to 9/10 of the time.**

**However**:
- When it fails, it fails CATASTROPHICALLY (70-94% losses)
- DCA 2x wins rarely, but saves you in disasters
- Non-Leveraged never wins often, but is the safest baseline

**Best overall approach**:
1. **If confident**: Lump-Sum 2x Leverage
2. **If uncertain**: DCA 2x Leverage
3. **If scared**: Non-Leveraged

The data clearly shows **leverage works most of the time**, but the **disasters are severe enough** that risk management (DCA or non-leveraged) has real value.
