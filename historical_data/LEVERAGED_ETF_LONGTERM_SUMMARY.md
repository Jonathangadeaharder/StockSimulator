## Why Long-Term Works (Usually)

### 1. **Time Smooths Volatility**
- Over 15-30 years, multiple market cycles average out
- Bull markets tend to last longer than bear markets
- Long uptrends overcome short crashes

### 2. **Compounding Dominates**
- Given positive long-term market returns (~10% historical)
- 2x daily leverage compounds to > 2x long-term returns
- Example: 18% unleveraged ‚Üí 36% leveraged annually

### 3. **Recovery After Crashes**
- Markets eventually recover from crashes
- Leveraged ETFs participate 2x in the recovery
- 1982-2000: Even with 1987 crash, leverage won hugely

### 4. **Volatility Decay Becomes Less Significant**
- Daily volatility effects average out over decades
- Long-term trend overwhelms short-term choppiness

## When Long-Term Leverage FAILS

### Requirements for 15-Year Underperformance:

1. **Multiple Major Crashes** - Need 2+ severe bear markets
2. **High Volatility Throughout** - Persistent choppiness
3. **Weak Recovery Periods** - Slow, choppy recoveries
4. **Poor Market Returns** - Unleveraged index barely positive

### Historical Examples:

**1998-2013 Period** (the worst):
- 2000-2002: Dot-com crash (-47% peak-to-trough)
- 2003-2007: Moderate recovery
- 2007-2009: Financial crisis (-57% peak-to-trough)
- 2009-2013: Recovery, but still recovering from double-crash

**Why it failed**:
- 15 years, but only 4.38% annual return (below historical average)
- Two 50%+ crashes with choppy recoveries
- High volatility throughout entire period
